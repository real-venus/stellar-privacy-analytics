# Requirements Document

## Introduction

The Privacy Impact Communication Tool provides a system for generating, distributing, and tracking privacy impact communications directed at stakeholders. It produces reports, visualizations, and plain-language explanations of the privacy implications of data processing activities — drawing on the existing `PrivacyPolicyEngine`, `AuditLoggingService`, `PrivacyBudgetService`, and the compliance workflows defined in the Privacy Policy Management system. The tool enables privacy officers, compliance teams, and data stewards to communicate privacy risk clearly and consistently across audiences, languages, and output formats, while maintaining a tamper-evident history of every communication sent.

---

## Glossary

- **Communication_Tool**: The system described in this document — the backend services and API endpoints for generating, distributing, and tracking privacy impact communications.
- **Privacy_Impact_Report**: A structured document that describes the privacy implications of one or more data processing activities, generated by the Communication_Tool.
- **Processing_Activity**: A named, described data operation (e.g., a query, a batch job, a data transfer) whose privacy implications are being communicated.
- **Stakeholder**: A named recipient of a Privacy_Impact_Report, identified by role (e.g., `data-subject`, `dpo`, `legal`, `executive`, `regulator`).
- **Communication_Template**: A versioned, parameterised document skeleton used to produce a Privacy_Impact_Report for a specific Stakeholder role and language.
- **Report_Renderer**: The subsystem that merges a Communication_Template with report data to produce a final Privacy_Impact_Report in a requested output format.
- **Visualization_Engine**: The subsystem that generates charts, risk matrices, and summary graphics embedded in Privacy_Impact_Reports.
- **Communication_Record**: An immutable entry in the Communication_History that captures the content, recipients, timestamp, and delivery status of a sent Privacy_Impact_Report.
- **Communication_History**: The append-only, audit-logged store of all Communication_Records.
- **Compliance_Workflow**: The policy lifecycle workflow defined in the Privacy Policy Management system, including validation, deployment, and rollback events.
- **Privacy_Budget**: The differential privacy epsilon budget tracked by `PrivacyBudgetService` for a given dataset.
- **Supported_Language**: A BCP-47 language tag (e.g., `en`, `fr`, `de`, `es`, `ja`) for which the Communication_Tool provides translated Communication_Templates.
- **Export_Format**: One of the supported output formats: `PDF`, `HTML`, or `JSON`.
- **Privacy_Officer**: A user with the `privacy-officer` role who can generate and send Privacy_Impact_Reports.
- **Report_Viewer**: A user with the `report-viewer` role who can read Privacy_Impact_Reports addressed to their Stakeholder role.
- **Report_Admin**: A user with the `report-admin` role who can manage Communication_Templates and configure Stakeholder mappings.

## Requirements

### Requirement 1: Automated Report Generation

**User Story:** As a Privacy_Officer, I want to generate a Privacy_Impact_Report for a Processing_Activity automatically, so that I can communicate privacy implications to stakeholders without manually authoring each report.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. THE Communication_Tool SHALL provide an API endpoint that accepts a Processing_Activity description (name, description, data categories, legal basis, retention period, and associated policy IDs) and generates a Privacy_Impact_Report.
2. WHEN a Privacy_Officer requests report generation, THE Communication_Tool SHALL retrieve the current policy evaluation results from `PrivacyPolicyEngine` for the specified policy IDs and incorporate them into the report.
3. WHEN a Privacy_Officer requests report generation, THE Communication_Tool SHALL retrieve the Privacy_Budget status from `PrivacyBudgetService` for any datasets referenced in the Processing_Activity and include budget consumption metrics in the report.
4. WHEN a Privacy_Officer requests report generation, THE Communication_Tool SHALL retrieve the compliance status of the referenced policies from the Privacy Policy Management system and include per-framework compliance results in the report.
5. THE Privacy_Impact_Report SHALL include: a risk summary, a list of data categories processed, the legal basis, applicable compliance framework statuses, privacy budget consumption (where applicable), and a plain-language explanation of privacy implications.
6. THE Report_Renderer SHALL complete report generation within 10 000 milliseconds for a Processing_Activity referencing up to 20 policies and 10 datasets.
7. WHEN report generation fails due to an unavailable upstream service, THE Communication_Tool SHALL return an HTTP 503 response identifying the unavailable dependency and SHALL record the failure in the Audit_Trail via `AuditLoggingService.logEvent()`.

---

### Requirement 2: Visual Privacy Impact Summaries

**User Story:** As a Privacy_Officer, I want Privacy_Impact_Reports to include visual summaries of privacy risk, so that non-technical stakeholders can quickly understand the key findings.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. THE Visualization_Engine SHALL generate a risk matrix chart that plots each identified privacy risk by likelihood (low, medium, high) and impact (low, medium, high) for inclusion in every Privacy_Impact_Report.
2. THE Visualization_Engine SHALL generate a privacy budget consumption bar chart showing current epsilon usage versus maximum epsilon for each referenced dataset.
3. THE Visualization_Engine SHALL generate a compliance status summary graphic showing pass/fail counts per registered Compliance_Framework.
4. WHEN a Privacy_Impact_Report is exported in `PDF` or `HTML` format, THE Report_Renderer SHALL embed all Visualization_Engine outputs as inline SVG or rasterized PNG images within the document.
5. WHEN a Privacy_Impact_Report is exported in `JSON` format, THE Report_Renderer SHALL include visualization data as structured JSON objects (risk matrix entries, budget figures, compliance counts) rather than image data.
6. THE Visualization_Engine SHALL produce all charts for a single Privacy_Impact_Report within 3 000 milliseconds.
7. IF the Privacy_Budget data for a referenced dataset is unavailable, THEN THE Visualization_Engine SHALL render the budget chart with a clearly labelled "data unavailable" placeholder rather than omitting the chart.

---

### Requirement 3: Stakeholder-Specific Communication Templates

**User Story:** As a Report_Admin, I want to define and manage Communication_Templates tailored to specific Stakeholder roles, so that each audience receives a report written at the appropriate level of technical detail.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. THE Communication_Tool SHALL provide built-in Communication_Templates for the following Stakeholder roles: `data-subject`, `dpo`, `legal`, `executive`, and `regulator`.
2. WHEN a Privacy_Officer generates a Privacy_Impact_Report for a specific Stakeholder role, THE Report_Renderer SHALL select the Communication_Template registered for that role and language combination.
3. IF no Communication_Template exists for the requested role and language combination, THEN THE Report_Renderer SHALL fall back to the `en` (English) template for that role; IF no English template exists for that role, THEN THE Communication_Tool SHALL return an HTTP 404 response.
4. THE Communication_Tool SHALL provide an API endpoint for Report_Admins to create, update, and delete custom Communication_Templates, accepting a role, BCP-47 language tag, template body (with named placeholders), and a version identifier.
5. WHEN a Report_Admin updates a Communication_Template, THE Communication_Tool SHALL create a new template version and preserve the previous version unchanged; existing Communication_Records SHALL reference the template version used at generation time.
6. THE Communication_Tool SHALL validate that all named placeholders in a submitted Communication_Template body are resolvable from the Privacy_Impact_Report data model before storing the template; IF unresolvable placeholders are found, THEN THE Communication_Tool SHALL return an HTTP 422 response listing the unresolvable placeholder names.
7. WHEN a Communication_Template is created, updated, or deleted, THE Communication_Tool SHALL record the event in the Audit_Trail via `AuditLoggingService.logEvent()` with the actor ID, template ID, and template version.

---

### Requirement 4: Multi-Language Support

**User Story:** As a Privacy_Officer, I want to generate Privacy_Impact_Reports in multiple languages, so that I can communicate with stakeholders in their preferred language.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. THE Communication_Tool SHALL support report generation in the following Supported_Languages at minimum: `en` (English), `fr` (French), `de` (German), `es` (Spanish), and `ja` (Japanese).
2. WHEN a Privacy_Officer requests a Privacy_Impact_Report with a specified BCP-47 language tag, THE Report_Renderer SHALL render all template-driven text sections in the requested language.
3. THE Communication_Tool SHALL store all built-in Communication_Templates in each Supported_Language independently, so that translations can be updated without affecting other languages.
4. WHEN a Privacy_Officer requests a language that is not a Supported_Language, THE Communication_Tool SHALL return an HTTP 400 response listing the supported BCP-47 language tags.
5. THE Communication_Tool SHALL render numeric values, dates, and currency amounts in the locale conventions of the requested language (e.g., decimal separators, date formats).
6. WHEN a Communication_Template for a Supported_Language is missing a translation for a specific section, THE Communication_Tool SHALL substitute the `en` text for that section and include a `translationFallback` flag in the JSON response identifying the affected sections.

---

### Requirement 5: Multi-Format Export

**User Story:** As a Privacy_Officer, I want to export Privacy_Impact_Reports in PDF, HTML, and JSON formats, so that I can share them through different channels and integrate them with other systems.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. THE Report_Renderer SHALL export any generated Privacy_Impact_Report in `PDF`, `HTML`, and `JSON` Export_Formats upon request.
2. WHEN a Privacy_Officer requests a `PDF` export, THE Report_Renderer SHALL produce a standards-compliant PDF/A-1b document containing all report sections and embedded visualizations.
3. WHEN a Privacy_Officer requests an `HTML` export, THE Report_Renderer SHALL produce a self-contained HTML5 document with inline CSS and embedded SVG visualizations that renders correctly without external resources.
4. WHEN a Privacy_Officer requests a `JSON` export, THE Report_Renderer SHALL produce a JSON document conforming to the Privacy_Impact_Report schema, including all structured data fields and visualization data objects.
5. FOR ALL valid Privacy_Impact_Reports, exporting to `JSON` and then re-importing the JSON to generate a new report SHALL produce a Privacy_Impact_Report that is semantically equivalent to the original (round-trip property).
6. THE Report_Renderer SHALL complete export of a Privacy_Impact_Report to any single Export_Format within 15 000 milliseconds.
7. WHEN an export request specifies an unsupported format, THE Communication_Tool SHALL return an HTTP 400 response listing the supported Export_Formats.

---

### Requirement 6: Compliance Workflow Integration

**User Story:** As a Privacy_Officer, I want Privacy_Impact_Reports to be automatically triggered and linked to compliance workflow events from the Privacy Policy Management system, so that stakeholders are notified whenever a policy change affects their data.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. WHEN the Privacy Policy Management system records a policy deployment event, THE Communication_Tool SHALL receive the event and automatically generate a Privacy_Impact_Report for each Stakeholder role configured to receive notifications for that policy's data categories.
2. WHEN the Privacy Policy Management system records a policy rollback event, THE Communication_Tool SHALL generate a Privacy_Impact_Report describing the rollback, the reason (if provided), and the restored policy version.
3. THE Communication_Tool SHALL provide an API endpoint for Report_Admins to configure Stakeholder notification rules, specifying which Stakeholder roles receive automatic reports for which policy IDs or data categories.
4. WHEN an automatically triggered Privacy_Impact_Report is generated, THE Communication_Tool SHALL link the Communication_Record to the originating Compliance_Workflow event ID from the Privacy Policy Management system's Audit_Trail.
5. WHEN a compliance workflow event triggers report generation and the generation fails, THE Communication_Tool SHALL retry generation up to 3 times with exponential backoff before recording a permanent failure in the Audit_Trail and emitting a Prometheus counter increment on the `pic_report_generation_failures_total` metric.
6. THE Communication_Tool SHALL expose a Prometheus-compatible metrics endpoint reporting: `pic_reports_generated_total` (by stakeholder role and format), `pic_reports_sent_total` (by stakeholder role), `pic_report_generation_duration_seconds` (histogram), and `pic_report_generation_failures_total` (by trigger type).

---

### Requirement 7: Historical Communication Tracking

**User Story:** As a Privacy_Officer, I want a searchable history of all Privacy_Impact_Reports that have been generated and sent, so that I can demonstrate accountability and retrieve past communications during audits.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. THE Communication_Tool SHALL create a Communication_Record for every Privacy_Impact_Report that is generated, capturing: report ID, Processing_Activity ID, Stakeholder role, language, Export_Format, generation timestamp, actor ID, template ID and version, and a SHA-256 hash of the report content.
2. THE Communication_History SHALL be append-only; existing Communication_Records SHALL be immutable after creation.
3. THE Communication_Tool SHALL anchor each Communication_Record hash to the Stellar blockchain via `AuditLoggingService.logEvent()` within 5 000 milliseconds of report generation completing.
4. THE Communication_Tool SHALL provide an API endpoint for querying the Communication_History, supporting filters by: Processing_Activity ID, Stakeholder role, date range, language, Export_Format, and triggering compliance workflow event ID.
5. WHEN a Privacy_Officer queries the Communication_History, THE Communication_Tool SHALL return results within 3 000 milliseconds for result sets of up to 10 000 Communication_Records.
6. THE Communication_History SHALL retain all Communication_Records for a minimum of 7 years to satisfy audit retention requirements.
7. WHEN a Report_Admin requests a Communication_Record by ID, THE Communication_Tool SHALL return the record including a verification status indicating whether the stored content hash matches the blockchain-anchored hash.
8. THE Communication_Tool SHALL provide an API endpoint that returns a summary count of Communications sent per Stakeholder role, per language, and per Export_Format for a specified date range, completing within 2 000 milliseconds for date ranges spanning up to 1 year.
